Cecelia Manguerra Brainard, fondly known as Cecelib, is a renowned Filipina author, editor, and literary advocate whose works have significantly shaped contemporary Philippine and Filipino American literature. Born in Cebu, Philippines, she is best known for her critically acclaimed novel When the Rainbow Goddess Wept (formerly Song of Yvonne), which has been widely read and taught around the world.

Cecelib’s writing often explores themes of war, identity, diaspora, culture, and the resilience of women. Her storytelling blends Filipino folklore with historical narrative, giving voice to generations of Filipinos both in the homeland and abroad. Aside from her novels, she has written short stories, essays, and children’s books, and has edited several anthologies showcasing Filipino and Asian American writers.

She is also the founder of Philippine American Literary House (PALH), a press dedicated to promoting Philippine literature. With her commitment to preserving and celebrating Filipino voices, Cecelib continues to inspire readers, educators, and fellow writers globally.
